Discover: Genomic Data Search

: User Research : Prototyping : Usability Testing

Summary

Designing a web application to help researchers with finding human Genomic data for their research. The project was in very early stage and lots of insights had to be gathered to understand the problems.

Problem

Discovery of Human genomic data is very difficult and time consuming as data are often hidden and scattered all over the world. This undervalue their high potential for reusaublity which could make the research process faster and cheaper.

Additionally, the quality of genomic data can be very poor, especially data sets from publicly available repositories. Data models can be very complex and inconsistent across different repositories what reflects on an overwhelming interface.

Overwhelming interface and inconsistency in data

Research

We conducted contextual interviews to see how human genomics researchers currently solve their problem with finding data: with a focus on how they search for data – current habits. What tools they use to make their work easier and What their greatest pain points and frustrations are.

We've created a group of volunteers mostly from academia who were available for the interviews and usability testings in future. We called them User Council link to user council.

Findings

Big pharma companies often have enough of their own data and are not depended on public repositories. Researchers do exploratory and directional searches depending on which stage of the research they are.

Design

Drive user behaviour to connect with community and contribute. Using the methology of Story mapping we’ve mapped out user journeys for these social features: notification, reputation, inviting friend, annotating datasets, following

For the search functionality the UI was redesigned to support exporatory and targeted search.
