Discover: Genomic Data Search

: User Research : Prototyping : Usability Testing

Summary

Discovery is a platform which goal is to help researchers with finding the right human genomic data for their research. By introducing the set of community and annotation features we would like to connect users together and enable to contribute with valuable information to improve the quality of the current data.

| About Repositive

Repositive is trying to solve problems with data quality and discoverablity in the field of genomic research. Their Discovery platform is aggregating datasets from multiple repositories and displays information in more consistent and digestible way. People can sign up for free and use powerful search to find relevant datasets.

Problem

The quality of genomic data can be very poor, especially data sets from publicly available repositories. Data models are very complex and inconsistent across different repositories. Finding relevant datasets for a research is very time-consuming and daunting.
Often researchers has to download big files to asses the quality and relevance of data.

Overwhelming interface and inconsistency in data

Who are the users

Genomic researchers and scientist who using genomic data to validate their hypothesis. The target user groups are Academics (Phd Students, PostDoc) and Scientists working in Pharma companies or in Biotech.

Typical behaviour and pain points of our users

Research

There was no prior research on this project. We did many internal brainstorming sessions where the whole team was involved in generating ideas and we were conducting usability testings during the design and development phase.

Solution

Build a set of features which will allow users to explore activity on the platform and encourage them on collaboration to create useful content. Empower and motivate users to become 'Repositive Champions’. The final list of features was: Notification, Reputation score, Metadata curation, Follow a dataset, User profile (adding interests, and social accounts), Self allocation of dataset, Recommentation, Searching trought other than data sets (users, interests), Create collection, Invite users, Upvote/Downvote, Request Help.

The scoreboard to track the development process

Designs

For the basic features we skipped the high-fidelity mockups and provided only paper sketches. To avoid any misunderstanding when implementing the features we communicated closely the flow through the sketches to dev team and used github for any additional input. Sometimes we video recorded the developer going through the flow to make sure all steps are interpreted correctly.

Videos for dev to communicate the designs

We have chosen story mapping to capture the ideas and steps of the user journey. I think it’s very effective method to get the whole picture of the feature and the minimum flow to achieve good user experience. Additionally, it’s nice to capture and explore more ideas which we were able to divide into future releases.

Story mapping and user journeys

Every week we had remote usability testings with people from our User Council where we tested basic interactions of the prototypes, mainly to ensure the terminology and intended functionality makes sense. I learnt that scenario you give the user is very important on the tester background. They should be familiar with the scenario, especial if the tested feature is on very specialised subject (e.g curating and annotating categories of a datasets).

Outcomes and learnings

Within 6 months we managed to develop 5 of 10 faetures we planned. We over estimated the scope of the features but the process worked quite well. Although general features (Notification, Edit Profile or Dataset) works quite well individually others like User Reputation would need more holistic integration to provide good user experience.

Usability Testing

During developing better Search functionality I spent most of time conducting Usability tests. These were mainly to validate our concepts on filtering, data sets layout and browsing. The designs were showed through video as a user story which was very effective to communicate the concept. However it wouldn't capture all usability issues which could appear when interacting with the search directly.

My main responsibility was to conduct the UX sessions and analyse the results and present to the team.

Research

Before start improving designs for Search we conducted contextual interviews to see how human genomics researchers in pharma currently solve their problem with finding data. We needed more insight into data search of users who work in industry setup. The big challenge was in recruiting participants as the employees are very bind with high confidentiality policies.

I was in charge of recruitment, scheduling and conducting the interviews. Following with data analysis and reporting to the product team.
